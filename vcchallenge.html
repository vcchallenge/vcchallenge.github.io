Introduction

We intend to gather technology that improves video compression beyond the DLVC with or without neural networks. Efficient video coding technology is the foundation and indispensable part of the image and signal processing field. Several series of video coding standards have been crafted in the past three decades, and several new ones are in the way coming. Techniques such as block partitioning, prediction mode and transform are becoming more sophisticated, and traditional video coding technology still shows strong vitality. With the development of deep learning and the proliferation of special hardware (e.g., GPUs), the method of recurrent neural network and convolutional neural network has been used to prove the potential of learning-based methods in the field of video compression.

Deep Learning-Based Video Coding (DLVC) features a set of deep learning-based coding techniques as well as new or improved non-learning-based coding techniques. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first open-source video coding software that integrates deep learning-based techniques into the traditional hybrid coding framework. It is quite promising: DLVC version 1.0 achieves more than 40% bits saving than H.265/HEVC at the same quality level! DLVC's outstanding performance and open source attributes make it an excellent platform for researching new video coding technologies.

This challenge is organized by the Future Video Coding Study Group (FVC-SG), which is affiliated with IEEE 1857 working group. We hope to attract researchers in the multimedia field, especially young students, to pay more attention to image and video compression. The winning program in the challenge will be recommended to the IEEE 1857 working group of video codec technical standards.

Challenge Tasks

  • Task1: Video Compression Challenge

The organizer welcomes any type of video compression technology. Considering the diversity of the coding platform, the organizer does not make any requirements for the coding platform and provides four baselines: x264, HM16.20, Vtm5.0 and DLVC. The challenge has two tracks.

HCP Track: HCP means high compression performance. In this track, there are no restrictions on the compression technology. All contestants participate in the track by default. Participants only need to ensure a certain degree of complexity so that the solution can be tested within the specified time.

HDE Track: HDE means high decoding efficiency. In this track, decoding efficiency will be an important indicator. The solution with a decoding complexity of no more than 50 times (CPU) of VTM5.0 will be automatically included in this track.

Submission Session: Participants need to package the technical solution into an executable file or docker file. In addition to this, runtime required files such as library files and trained models also need to be submitted simultaneously. All submitted files should be packaged and sent to vcchallenge@163.com.

Testing Session: The organizer will conduct a unified test of all tasks and submit the test results to the FVC meeting. All test results will be released on the Internet in time. Specific test conditions can be downloaded. The test set will not be published, but we will provide test results on three baselines for reference.

Evaluation Session: Overall BD Rate and Complexity will be the main indicators of evaluation, but the focus of the two tracks is different. HCP track is more concerned with compression performance, and HDE track has higher requirements for complexity. Participants will have the opportunity to present their advancement, novelty or other superior characteristics at the FVC meeting. The final score of the two tracks will be assessed by the FVC expert group.

The awards process of this challenge will be synchronized with the FVC meeting held in HaiKou in Aug 2019. Everyone is welcome to participate actively. The specific meeting venue will be announced later.

  • Task2: Open Source Competition

In order to promote in-depth communication and technology sharing in this field of image and video compression, the organizer will organize an open source competition. We hope to provide a platform for researchers in the field to showcase, communicate and share their work.

The organizer welcomes any articles published in international journals and international conferences in the field of image and video coding in the past two years. If you are willing to share your work and promise to open source the code related to the articles, the organizer will welcome you and you will have the chance to win a certain bonus.

Each participant will need to provide a poster to describe the technical solution of the article and related open source information. In addition, participants have three minutes to briefly introduce their work and contributions. The FVC expert group will select some excellent sharing and issue certificates and bonuses.
